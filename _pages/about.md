Welcome to my website! I'm a Predoctoral Research Fellow at [Microsoft Research's Economics & Computation Group](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/theme/economics-and-computation/) currently working on projects at the intersection of labor economics, behavioral economics, and game theory. Before that, I worked for a year as a Data Analyst at One Drop and graduated from UC Berkeley in 2021 with a double major in Economics and Computer Science. In the Fall of 2024, I will be starting a PhD in Economics at Brown University.  

I am highly interested in studying both the drivers of technology and the impacts of modern technology on human decision-making. Current and past research that I have been involved with includes projects related to how AI has affected jobs in the healthcare industry, the spread of misinformation in social networks, understanding how users respond to streaming-related ads on search engines, modeling biased belief formation in the presence of unique and repeated information, designing optimal autobidders, solving for equilibrium beliefs in social networks with polarized users and platform moderation, and measuring labor turnover in marekts with state-funded worker training grants. Outside of work I enjoy playing soccer and basketball, philosophy, listening to music, and stand up comedy. 

You can reach me at **sidsatya.research@gmail.com**. Thanks for visiting!
